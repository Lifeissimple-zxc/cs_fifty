Video games have been the biggest motivation for me to learn the English language which in turn enabled me to get into IT and discover my passion for working with code.

Crisis Core: Final Fantasy VII

"Embrace your dreams...and whatever happens, protect your honor" ©

When playing this game as a kid with B1 English at max, I wanted to understand the plot so hard that I would translate some phrases using a dictionary. Reflecting on it now, Crisis Core has taught me learning can be combined with pleasure and yield results.

Playing the Reunion version disappointed me due to the voice acting. My personal recommendation here would be to go for the original PSP version, but that's a tough sell for many and I understand that.

Final Fantasy Series

Originating in 1987 and being the last hope of its creator, Hironobu Sakaguchi, it proves that having dreams and following them can require making a leap of faith.

Due to the series having many installations, I cannot focus on a particular game. However, the things that unite all the FF titles are:

  • Memorable characters with memorable personalities and well-written motivation.
  • Terrific music, Nobuo Uematsu is a genius!
  • Ahead of time graphics.
  • Read the pages' content, see if it resonates with you.

Crisis core was the game that opened this series for me and I am a devoted fan of it still.

Kingdom Hearts Series

This game combines all the strong points of Final Fantasy, Disney characters we all love and wraps it into an action game.

Initially, the project caused many doubts, but similar to FF, the creators took a leap of faith and followed their dreams and ambitions which as of today results in 20+ years of history, 10+ games and millions of fans all over the world.

The main protagonist values friendship above all, is very dedicate and remains positive in the toughest situations. When the times are dark, I look up to Sora and the creators of Kingdom Hearts.
