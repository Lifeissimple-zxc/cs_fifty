Overall, reading is something I need to allocate more time to, but the following works are definitely my top of mind when it comes to reading.

Clockwork Orange

The book reminded me that reading can be joyful. Following Alex as he forcefully undergoes an enforced transformation from a complete villain into a peaceful pawn with no will made me remember how integral having choice is (even if what on chooses is not "good" according to the majority's norms)

The style of writing is unique and Alex's taste for Ludwig Van motivated me to revisit the works of this great composer.

For me this reading falls under the "Entertainment category", but it definitely is not one of guilty pleasure type!

Amazon Link

Using Asyncio in Python

Shortly after joining Uber, I was expected to take over some async programs written in Python. Following videos on Youtube got me familiar with the library's syntax, buy brought no understanding of WHY async code needs to be written this way

Caleb Hattingh mamanges to explain the concepts of async programming by providing simple (and sometimes not as much) examples supported by code snippets. The author also gives enough context on the ways via which developers can leverage Python's capabilities for concurrency.

Reading this book made me less afraid of async and now I am able to write async programs in Python programming language.

Amazon Link

Platform Revolution

This reading was recommended by the (at the time) VP of Delivery business of Bolt where I was employed back then.

Reading it enabled me to better understand the dynamics of on-demand delivery business (although I am still learning about it...). It explains the concepts of network effects, the examples refer to real-world companies and the conclusions are concise yet informative.

Although there might be better resources covering platform business model, this was the one that introduced it to me.
